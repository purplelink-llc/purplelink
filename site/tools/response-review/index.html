# Response to Reviewers Review

## Per-Comment Tracker

| # | Reviewer comment | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| R1.2 | "Sample size seems small for the claimed effect." | Addressed. Power analysis added to Section 3.2. |
| R1.4 | "Figure 3 axis labels are unreadable." | Partially addressed. Labels enlarged, but the legend still overlaps the plot. |
| R2.1 | "The related-work section ignores prior work by Chen et al." | Hand-waved. Response says "we are aware of this work" without adding a citation or comparison. |
| R2.3 | "Why not compare against baseline X?" | Rejected with argument. Response explains X is not applicable to this setting; reasoning is sound. |

## Tone Concerns

- Response to R2.1 reads as dismissive: "As any reader familiar with
  the field would understand, this comparison is unnecessary." Suggest:
  "We agree this comparison is worth addressing directly. [reasoning]"

## Missing Responses

- R1.5 ("Please clarify the exclusion criteria for Experiment 2")
  has no corresponding response letter entry.

## Action Checklist

- [A] Add a citation and one-sentence comparison to Chen et al. for R2.1.
- [A] Write a response to the missing R1.5 comment.
- [B] Fix the overlapping legend in Figure 3.

What the report includes

  1. Per-comment tracker. Every reviewer comment, paired with your response, marked as addressed, partially addressed, hand-waved, rejected with argument, or not evaluable.
  2. Tone concerns. Defensive, dismissive, or argumentative phrasing the reviewer will notice. Suggested rewrites that say the same thing less abrasively.
  3. Missing responses. Reviewer comments you didn't address at all (it happens, easy to miss one).
  4. Action checklist. A/B/C-prioritised list of what to fix in the response letter or manuscript before hitting submit.

FAQ

What format should the reviewer comments be in?
Paste them as plain text. Common patterns are auto-detected: "Reviewer 1:" headers, "(1)" or "1." numbered comments. If your editor sent them as a PDF, copy the text out.
And the response letter?
Same — paste the draft text. We don't accept a PDF for the response.
Does the manuscript have to be the revised version?
Yes. We check whether the response's claimed fixes are actually present in the revision.
What does Purplelink retain?
Nothing after delivery. The report is deleted from our servers the moment you retrieve it. Your reviewer comments, response letter, and manuscript are sent to Anthropic's Claude API, which retains inputs for 30 days for abuse monitoring — never for training.
