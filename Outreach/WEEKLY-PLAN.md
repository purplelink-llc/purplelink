## What to skip if pressed for time

In order of "first to skip":

1. **Bluesky posts beyond week 5** — the platform is small enough that 5 strong posts establish presence; weekly cadence is nice-to-have not essential.
2. **LibGuide emails 6–8** — diminishing returns after the 5 named contacts.
3. **Reddit posts** — only worth it when a relevant thread appears organically. Don't force it.
4. **Original LinkedIn posts** — the template content for weeks 1–4 covers the must-have signal.

The two things to NOT skip:

1. **Identity setup in Week 1** — without LinkedIn + GitHub + Bluesky, none of the other work signals back to your brand.
2. **TeX SE answers** — the highest-leverage long-term move for the tools cluster.

## When to update the toolkit

- **Quarterly**: re-run target discovery (TeX SE has new questions; LibGuides update). Spawn the discovery agent again.
- **Per launch**: per the launch kits.
- **When a campaign converts**: if a LinkedIn post drives unexpected traffic, write a follow-up version.

---

## What success looks like at 90 days

Realistic expectations for a 5-week-old domain on this cadence:

- **Referring domains**: 8–15 (Chrome Web Store, your LinkedIn / GitHub / Bluesky, 2–3 LibGuide inclusions, 1–2 community pages)
- **TeX SE reputation**: 200–400 (assumes ~6 answers, half upvoted)
- **LinkedIn followers**: 50–150
- **Bluesky followers**: 100–300
- **Direct site referrals from community**: 30–100/month by week 12

These are *small numbers* by SaaS standards but exactly right for a one-person studio building a craft audience. Compound growth comes from each placement → 1–2 more discoveries → and so on.
