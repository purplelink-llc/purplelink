What's inside

Everything an academic needs, nothing they don't

Submission tracking

Log every journal and conference submission with status, dates, and decisions. Get a realistic forecast of publication timelines based on your submission history.

Grant management

Track NSF, NIH, and other grants — deadlines, budgets, personnel, and progress — in one place. Import NSF grant data automatically.

CV & packages

Your CV populates automatically from your project and submission data. Export to PDF, LaTeX, or Word when you need it.

Inbox triage

Paste or sync email from Gmail and Outlook. Vitae's on-device parser reads your correspondence and proposes submission updates — you review before anything changes.

Collaborators & projects

Track coauthors, collaborators, and projects. Link people to papers, grants, and roles so your network stays navigable.

Frequently asked questions

Is Vitae really free?
Yes. Vitae is free to download and use with no trial period, no subscription, and no feature gates. If you find it useful, a tip via Buy Me a Coffee is appreciated but never required.
Why isn't it on the Mac App Store?
Distributing outside the App Store lets me ship faster and avoid App Store restrictions. The DMG is Apple-notarized, so macOS Gatekeeper verifies it is safe to run — just open the DMG, drag Vitae to Applications, and launch it.
What macOS version is required?
Vitae requires macOS 15 Sequoia or later.
Where is my data stored?
All data is stored locally on your Mac using SwiftData. Nothing is sent to any server. iCloud sync is supported in the schema and can be enabled in future versions.
How do I install it?
Download the DMG, open it, drag Vitae into your Applications folder, and launch it. Because it is notarized by Apple, you will not see a Gatekeeper warning.
