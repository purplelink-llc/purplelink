The Faceless Content Pipeline

Most faceless-video tools ask a language model to make something up and hope a moderator does not notice. That is fine for cat facts and a liability in health, finance, legal, or supplements. This is the opposite approach.

The pipeline builds each short-form video from a source you choose, summarises only what the source actually says, and adds one short, pre-approved takeaway from a vocabulary you define. It narrates, captions, brands, and renders the video, then drips it to TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and Instagram Reels on a schedule. It was built and hardened on a live channel that survived a real platform moderation review, and that de-risk playbook is included.

What it does differently

Source-only summaries

The summariser uses only what the source states. If the source gives no number, the script has no number. This removes the single largest risk in automated content: the confident invented fact.

A pre-approved takeaway, with a hard filter

The one place a video sounds actionable is the takeaway line, and it may only draw from a short, closed list you define. Every generated line is run through a ban filter before rendering. If a line reads as real advice, it is dropped, never quietly reworded. A source that maps to nothing safe gets no takeaway.

Disclosure baked in

An on-screen and spoken disclosure is part of every clip, so you are not relying on a platform toggle that does not cover outside affiliate links.

How you use it

  1. Install the pipeline and its dependencies (Python and ffmpeg, both free).
  2. Point it at your source. A working feed adapter ships in the box; your own is a fifteen-line template.
  3. Define your safe takeaways and your banned phrases. This is the boundary the engine will not cross.
  4. Add your logo, background clips, and a voice.
  5. Connect each platform once with the login helper.
  6. Dry-run to watch the first videos, then schedule the drip.

Plan on about twenty minutes end to end. Most of it is pasting credentials. You do not need to write code.

What you get: the full pipeline, the compliance layer, a source-adapter template, the moderation de-risk playbook, and a beautifully typeset setup guide. Everything runs locally on your own machine and your own API keys. Nothing phones home.

Who it is not for

It is delivered as-is: platform moderation outcomes and third-party pricing are outside anyone's control, and nothing here is legal advice.
