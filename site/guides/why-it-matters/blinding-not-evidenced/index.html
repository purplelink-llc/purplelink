blinding not evidenced

Claiming blinding without methods support.

What it is

Blinding — keeping the researcher or rater unaware of which condition a participant is in — controls for expectancy effects. A paper that asserts 'all experiments were performed blind to condition' without describing HOW gives reviewers no way to verify the claim. Common gaps: who labelled the samples? When was the unblinding key generated? Did the analyst know which tubes were which?

Why a reviewer cares

Reviewers ask: what specifically was blinded, when, and by whom? Was a blinding check performed (can the rater guess the condition above chance)? Where automated, what was the random assignment procedure?

How to fix it

Describe the blinding procedure in concrete terms: who randomized, who handled samples, when blinding was broken, whether an audit trail exists. If blinding was impossible (e.g. obvious physical differences), say so and discuss the threat.
