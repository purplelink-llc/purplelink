# University LibGuide outreach

Eight verified target LibGuides. Five have named-librarian contacts; three need a routing form. Goal per email: not a request to "add a link" — a useful note about a tool that genuinely fits their already-curated list, with no pressure.

## Tone rules

- **No subject like "Resource request for your LibGuide."** Read as spam.
- **Lead with what you saw on their page.** Specifically. Proves you read it.
- **Offer one concrete addition.** Not three. One link, max.
- **No follow-up.** If they don't respond, leave it. Libraries are over-emailed.
- **Sign from your real name + studio.** No marketing department voice.

## Outreach cadence

- Send max 2 per week. Spread Tuesday–Thursday morning, librarian local time.
- After sending: log in `OUTREACH-LOG.md` with date + target + which template was used.
- Wait 14 days. No follow-up. Move on.

---

## What to do if a librarian responds

- **"Thanks, we'll consider it"** — most common. No reply needed; let the inclusion happen naturally.
- **"Could you tell me more about [tool]?"** — they're considering. Reply with one paragraph and a screen-recording link if you have one. No high-pressure follow-up.
- **"We've added it"** — reply with a sincere thank-you. Don't ask them to do anything else.
- **"We don't add commercial tools"** — common at some libraries. Reply once, clarifying that your tools are free + no-signup, then move on if they still decline.
